The Weeknd

The Weeknd (real name Abel Tesfaye) was born and raised in Toronto, Canada. His birthday is Feb. 16, 1990, and his height is 5'8". He launched his career by anonymously uploading his music to YouTube, and released his first mixtape, 'House of Balloons,' in 2011; the project reached No. 113 on the Billboard 200. His debut studio album, 'Kiss Land,' came in September 2013 and peaked at No. 2, with 2018's 'My Dear Melancholy' EP becoming his first No. 1 album. "Wicked Games" was his debut single, and it reached a peak of No. 53 on the Hot 100 in 2013. Since then, he has had multiple No. 1 songs, including 2015's "The Hills" and "Can't Feel My Face," and "Save Your Tears" and "Die for You" with Ariana Grande. Other artists he's collaborated with include Drake, Dona Cat, Daft Punk, Maluma and Post Malone. He has earned multiple Grammys, including best urban contemporary album for 'Starboy' in 2018, and best R&B performance for "Earned It" in 2016.

‘XO Crew’


Why Do The Weeknd Fans In the past year, The Weeknd has truly permeated pop culture.His 2020 album, After Hours, spawned number-one hits such as “Heartless” and “Blinding Lights.” And the artist has made headlines for both his snub at the Grammys and his Super Bowl LV halftime show performance.Certainly, The Weeknd’s popularity is stronger than ever. But here’s why fans of his music call themselves the “XO Crew.” Whenever a celebrity develops a groundswell of devoted fans, they develop a name for themselves. Take Beyoncé’s “Beyhive,” for example, or Taylor Swift’s “Swifties.” These super-fans remain faithful to their respective artists through thick and thin. The Weeknd’s rise to fame was a lot more gradual than many realize. So it makes sense he’d have his own fandom name. The singer started on YouTube in 2010 before realizing a trilogy of mixtapes the following year. Even early on, The Weeknd — whose real name is Abel Tesfaye — began signing his social posts with his first name and an “xo.” Needless to say, that signature caught on both with his fans and the artist himself, who named his own record label imprint XO. While “XO” has been ingrained in The Weeknd’s career from the beginning, its exact meaning is unconfirmed. Typically, an “XO” signifies a hug and a kiss. But in the case of The Weeknd, many believe it has a more specific interpretation.
